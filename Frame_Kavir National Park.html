Kavir National Park, Iran




89,286 sq km Located in the central desert regions of iran, Kavir National Park features vast salt flats, sand dunes, and rugged mountains, home to rare species like the asiatic cheetah. Kavir National Park is a national park in northern Iran with an area of 4,000 km2 (1,500 sq mi)located on the western end of the Dasht-e Kavir desert. It encompasses desert and steppe landscapes harbouring native goats (Capra aegagrus), sheep (Ovis orientalis),striped hyenas, Indian wolves, gazelles, Asiatic cheetah and the Persian leopard.
